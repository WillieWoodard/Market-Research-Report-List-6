<p><p>Electric transmission and distribution equipment is categorized into three main types based on voltage levels: low voltage, medium voltage, and high voltage. Low voltage equipment, typically under 1 kV, is used for residential and commercial applications, distributing power to end-users. Medium voltage, ranging from 1 kV to 38 kV, serves industrial and utility needs, facilitating regional power distribution. High voltage equipment, exceeding 38 kV, is essential for long-distance transmission, ensuring efficient bulk electricity transfer across vast networks.</p></p>
