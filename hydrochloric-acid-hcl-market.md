<p><p>The hydrochloric acid (HCl) market is primarily divided into two types: synthetic hydrochloric acid and by-product hydrochloric acid. Synthetic hydrochloric acid is produced through direct methods, often from chemical processes such as the reaction of sodium chloride with sulfuric acid, yielding high purity HCl. By-product hydrochloric acid is generated as a secondary product in various industrial processes, such as the production of organic chemicals or soda ash. Each type serves different end-user industries, including metal processing, food production, and water treatment.</p></p>
