<p><p>The retinoblastoma treatment market is categorized into two main types: congenital (hereditary) and sporadic (non-hereditary) retinoblastoma. Congenital retinoblastoma is inherited and presents significant risks for siblings, necessitating targeted therapies such as genetic counseling and advanced surgical techniques. In contrast, sporadic retinoblastoma arises without genetic predisposition and often requires a different therapeutic approach, focusing on localized treatments like laser therapy and cryotherapy. Understanding these distinctions helps tailor treatment strategies effectively for affected patients.</p></p>
