<p><p>The dairy enzymes market comprises several key types, including lactase, chymosin, microbial rennet, and lipase. Lactase is primarily used to break down lactose in dairy products, catering to lactose-intolerant consumers. Chymosin, an essential enzyme in cheese production, helps coagulate milk. Microbial rennet serves as a vegetarian alternative to traditional animal rennet, facilitating cheese-making. Lipase enhances the flavor profile of dairy products by breaking down fats, contributing to the distinct taste of various cheeses. Together, these enzymes drive innovation and meet diverse consumer needs.</p></p>
