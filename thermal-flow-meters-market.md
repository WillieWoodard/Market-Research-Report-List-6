<p><strong>The Thermal Flow Meters Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Ultrasonic</li><li>Coriolis</li><li>Turbine</li><li>Vortex</li><li>Magnetic</li><li>Differential Pressure</li><li>Positive Displacement</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Thermal flow meters encompass various types including ultrasonic, coriolis, turbine, vortex, magnetic, differential pressure, and positive displacement. Ultrasonic flow meters use sound waves to measure fluid velocity, while coriolis meters assess mass flow through tube vibrations. Turbine meters utilize rotor movement to gauge flow, and vortex meters detect oscillations from flow disruptions. Magnetic flow meters apply Faraday’s principle to measure conductive fluids, whereas differential pressure sensors determine flow based on pressure differences. Positive displacement meters measure fluid volume through chamber displacement. Each type serves specific applications based on accuracy, fluid properties, and operational conditions.</p></p>
