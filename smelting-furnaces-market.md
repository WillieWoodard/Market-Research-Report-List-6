<p><p>Smelting furnaces are divided into two main types based on their heat source: combustion-type and electric-type. Combustion-type furnaces utilize fuels such as coal, gas, or oil to generate heat for smelting processes. These furnaces are typically cheaper to operate but may have higher maintenance costs. Electric-type furnaces, on the other hand, rely on electricity for heat generation. While they may have higher initial costs, they are often more efficient and environmentally friendly. Each type of furnace caters to different needs and preferences in the smelting industry.</p></p>
