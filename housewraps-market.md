<p><p>The housewrap market includes several types designed to enhance moisture management and energy efficiency in building construction. Asphalt-impregnated paper or fiberglass wraps provide a traditional barrier against moisture while allowing breathability. Micro-perforated wraps feature tiny holes that ventilate while blocking larger water droplets. Spunbonded nonwoven materials offer excellent tear resistance and flexibility. Woven wraps, typically made from synthetic fibers, provide strength and durability. Drainable house wraps are engineered to facilitate water drainage, reducing the risk of mold and damage.</p></p>
