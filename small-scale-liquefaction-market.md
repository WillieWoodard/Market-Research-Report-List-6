<p><p>The small-scale liquefaction market comprises two primary types: direct liquefaction and indirect liquefaction. Direct liquefaction involves converting biomass or organic materials directly into liquid fuels, typically through thermochemical processes like pyrolysis or gasification. In contrast, indirect liquefaction first converts feedstock into synthesis gas, which is then transformed into liquid fuels through chemical reactions. Both methods aim to provide renewable energy solutions, but they differ in their approach to feedstock processing and fuel production efficiency.</p></p>
