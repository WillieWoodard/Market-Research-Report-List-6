<p><ul><li>Contact Water Sensors</li><li>Non-contacting Water Sensors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The water sensors market is primarily divided into two types: contact and non-contact water sensors. Contact water sensors require direct physical contact with water to function, typically using probes to measure parameters like conductivity or water level. In contrast, non-contacting water sensors operate without direct interaction with water, utilizing technologies such as ultrasonic waves or radar to detect water presence and levels. Both types play essential roles in applications ranging from flood monitoring to industrial process control.</p></p>
