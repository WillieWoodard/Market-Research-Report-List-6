<p><ul><li>Mydriatic Fundus Camera</li><li>Non-Mydriatic Fundus Camera</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The desktop fundus camera market is segmented into mydriatic and non-mydriatic types. Mydriatic fundus cameras require pupil dilation using pharmacological agents, allowing for detailed imaging of the retina, which is essential for detecting conditions like diabetic retinopathy. In contrast, non-mydriatic fundus cameras do not require dilation, making them more convenient for quick screenings and easier to use in primary care settings. Both types play crucial roles in ophthalmology, facilitating early diagnosis and monitoring of eye diseases.</p></p>
