<p><ul><li>Infrared Sensor</li><li>Catalytic Bead Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The methane sensor market primarily consists of two types: infrared sensors and catalytic bead sensors. Infrared sensors use light absorption to detect methane concentrations, offering high accuracy and sensitivity. They are ideal for continuous monitoring in various environments. Conversely, catalytic bead sensors detect methane through oxidation reactions that generate heat, with their effectiveness reliant on the surrounding environment. While both types have diverse applications in industrial, environmental, and safety monitoring, infrared sensors are often favored for their precision and low maintenance needs.</p></p>
