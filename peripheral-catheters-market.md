<p><p>The peripheral catheters market is primarily divided into two types: Peripheral Intravenous Catheters (PIVC) and Midline Catheters. PIVCs are short-term devices used for intravenous access, typically inserted into small veins for medication administration and fluid delivery. In contrast, Midline Catheters are longer than PIVCs, designed for extended use, inserted into larger veins but not reaching the central circulation. These devices cater to different patient needs, influencing their application in healthcare settings based on duration and type of therapy.</p></p>
